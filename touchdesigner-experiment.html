Overview

A collection of real-time visual experiments built inside TouchDesigner, exploring generative motion, audio-reactive feedback, and live performance systems. Each artifact represents a different stage of the process — from midterm exploration to a fully realized event visual system.

Video Artifact 01

Midterm experimental project exploring real-time motion, feedback behavior, and abstract visual systems inside TouchDesigner.

Video Artifact 02

Video Artifact 03

Live event visual sequence demonstrating the audio reactive feedback system in a performance environment.

Process Notes

Each experiment began with a core feedback loop — a signal routed back into itself to produce emergent visual behavior. Parameters like gain, threshold, and timing were tuned in real-time to shape the output. Audio reactivity was introduced in the second artifact by mapping frequency bands to visual properties, creating a direct relationship between sound and motion.

Drawing inspiration from Char Davies' Osmose, I developed a series of interconnected digital landscapes using TouchDesigner. Audio-reactive systems controlled environmental changes, allowing the visuals to evolve organically alongside the music.

Outcome

The third artifact was performed live at a public exhibition at California State University, Fullerton. The system guided viewers through a narrative of emergence, transformation, and dissolution — combining procedural landscapes, reactive motion, and layered visual systems to transform the gallery space into a living digital environment.
